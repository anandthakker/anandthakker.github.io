Simple data explorer for Baltimore neighborhood data.

In response to a local challenge for this year’s National Day of Civic Hacking, designer Bryan Connor and I built a simple app for exploring a set of data about Baltimore neighborhoods. The data, curated/collected by BNIA, includes dozens of different indicators about 55 “Community Statistical Areas” in Baltimore.

The idea here is to make a way to explore indicators and neighborhoods of interest. Currently, you can choose your indicators from the menu on the right, and you’ll get a choropleth map and a histogram for each one, presented as “small multiples”. You can also highlight specific neighborhoods and rearrange the maps.

A work in progress, for sure, but here’s the current live version and the github repo.
